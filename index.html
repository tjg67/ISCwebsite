Mission

There is an increasing need for infrastructure sensing to enable the Internet of Things and Industry 4.0. The infrastructures are essential for the nation’s safe and secure operation, including power generation, energy transportation, power grid, civil structures, and renewables. Existing sensor technologies are not adequate to meet the increasing need for monitoring; therefore, new sensor technologies need to be developed. As the sensor technology matures, technology transfer and deployment become more important, which require engagement of industry and workforce development including training and educating highly skilled, interdisciplinary scientists and engineers capable of working at interfaces between disciplines. NETL-UPitt Infrastructure Sensing Consortium will focus on development and advancement of sensor technologies for critical infrastructure monitoring, spanning energy delivery and storage (e.g. natural gas, oil, H2), electricity grid, conventional and renewable energy generation, and carbon storage.

Next

Goals

1) Develop novel sensor technologies to pursue innovative solutions to infrastructure sensing needs 
2) Bolster NETL regional collaboration with UPitt and promote workforce development in the emerging sensor area to support both near-term R&D capability needs and future deployment and commercial needs;
3)Provide a platform to engage industry and stakeholders, receive feedback on sensor technologies, and provide innovative and practical technologies to industry via technology transfer; 
4) Establish a world-class and leading team and collaboration ready to respond to funding agencies’ and industry’s call for sensor technologies.

Next

Vision

Sensor Technology Will:

  1. Will encompass multiple sensing platforms (e.g. optical fiber sensing, passive wireless sensors, electrochemical sensors, chip sensors)
  2. Will be developed based on fundamental principles of sensor materials and demonstrated with prototypes in field validations,
  3. Include physics-based modeling and artificial intelligence (AI)-enabled sensor networks and digital twin. Impacts of intelligent infrastructure sensing include predictive monitoring before infrastructure failures occur (structural, electrical, etc.), mitigation of green-house gas emissions, enabling large-scale H2 transportation, supporting needs for a robust and resilient electricity and natural gas transportation and delivery system, and early detection of environmental contamination.
